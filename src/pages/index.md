### About the Author

My sojourn began with my native tongue, English, briefly flirted with the digital in school—amorous glances are still sometimes cast back this way—, and then entered what has proved to be its main part, Japanese. 

The imeptus for my turning East came in 2015 when I noticed a line in an anime episode that clearly consisted of a full sentence in the audio but whose subtitle contained only two words. "What are they really saying?" I wondered. "Is it truly so foreign that it cannot be rendered into English?"

From that day, I set about learning the language with an eye toward mastery. I read grammar guides, filled notebook after notebook with handwritten sinographs, studied flash cards, practiced on Japanese friends and acquaintances, and consumed as much Japanese media as I reasonably could. All this went through many cycles of great diligence followed by relative looseness, but the one invariant is that I never stopped engaging with the language.

My journey eventually took on a certain parallelity as I was drawn again toward the tongue of my forefathers. As I studied formal Japanese grammar, I realized that the concepts I learned there were also the tools to view my own language analytically. Though I had, and have, much confidence in my keen intuition, some things simply cannot be adequately explained through anything but a formal lens. Since then, I have dedicated a substantial amount of time to understanding the finer and obscurer points of English grammar, especially as it existed before certain forms were popularly conflated, confused, and extinguished.

This sojourn continues now and will until the end of my days, for that which may be learned infinitely exceeds the bounds of my earthly life. Whither I go, I know not. It may be that I will find myself someday in new and unexpected paths just as when I took my first step into Japanese. Wherever I end up, it is my hope to take what I have learned and return it in some form to the world, for knowledge without expression is dead even while its knower lives.
