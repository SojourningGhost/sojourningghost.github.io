---
title: 弥助に就いての記録の英訳
---

When the controversy concerning ｜弥助《Yasuke》 arose recently, I decided to investigate what the primary sources actually say about him. The following two excerpts are, as far as I can tell, the only Japanese records of his existence. All other records about him comes from Jesuit sources.

Since I have been questioned concerning this, let it be known I deliberately chose to represent the bluntness of 黒坊主 by translating it as *a black*. Though it is hard to gauge the exact level of respect intended since that is the first known historical use of the word, the presence of 坊主 in the compound alone indicates a certain and undeniable lack of politeness. It is essentially, at best, a plain description of his appearance (dark and possibly bald/shaven), not a polite appellation.

[信長公記](https://ja.wikisource.org/wiki/%E4%BF%A1%E9%95%B7%E5%85%AC%E8%A8%98#%E9%A6%96-%E5%8D%81%E5%9B%9B)
二月廿三日きりしたん国より黒坊主参り候年之齢廿六七と見えたり惣之身の黒き事牛之如彼男|健《すく》やかに器量也|爾《しか》も強力十之人に|勝《すぐれ》たり伴天連召列参御礼申上

The Nobunaga Chronicle
February 23rd. From the Christian country comes a black. He appears 26 or 27 years of age. The blackness of his whole body is like that of an ox. He is healthy and fit. Furthermore, he is strong, surpassing ten men. The priest brought him and came to pay his respects [to Nobunaga].

[家忠日記](https://blog.goo.ne.jp/kinosan1/m/201603)
上様、御扶持候、大うす進上申し候、くろ男御連れ候。
身は墨の如く、丈は六尺二分、名は弥介と云う。

Diary of Ietada
Lord [Nobunaga] brings with him the black man whom he retains and was given him by a |deus《priest》. [The black man's] body is like ink, his height is six _shaku_ and two _bu_ (5' 11.5" / 1.82m), and his name is called Yasuke.
